Appointment of Agent

I, ___, owner of the property located at ___, hereby appoint OverAssessed, LLC as my agent to represent me before the appraisal district and appraisal review board for all matters relating to the appraisal of the above-referenced property for property tax purposes.

This appointment authorizes the agent to:

  • Receive all notices and communications regarding the property from the appraisal district
  • File protests and any required documents on my behalf
  • Appear at informal hearings and formal Appraisal Review Board (ARB) hearings
  • Negotiate settlements and enter into agreements regarding the appraised value
  • Access property records maintained by the appraisal district

This appointment is effective immediately and remains in effect for the current tax year unless revoked in writing by the property owner.

Per Texas Tax Code § 1.111, a property owner may authorize an agent to represent them in property tax matters. An electronic signature on this form is legally binding under the Texas Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) and the federal ESIGN Act.

📋 Service Agreement

Property Tax Appeal Service Agreement

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between ___ ("Client") and OverAssessed, LLC ("Agent") for property tax appeal services related to the property located at ___ in ___ County, Georgia.

Scope of Services

Agent agrees to provide the following services on behalf of Client:

  • Analysis of the property's current assessed value against comparable sales data
  • Preparation and filing of Appeal Form PT-311A with the ___ County Board of Tax Assessors
  • Representation at informal hearings with the Board of Tax Assessors
  • Representation at formal Board of Equalization hearings, if necessary
  • All correspondence and communication with county tax officials

Fee Structure

  • Contingency Fee: 25% of actual Year 1 property tax savings achieved
  • No Upfront Costs: No admin fees, no hidden costs, no payment required before services are rendered
  • No Savings = No Fee: If OverAssessed does not achieve a reduction in your assessed value, you owe nothing
  • 3-Year Value Freeze: A successful appeal at a Board of Equalization hearing freezes your assessed value for 3 years under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-299(c). Our fee applies only to Year 1 savings — you keep 100% of Years 2 and 3

Important Disclosures

⚠️ Value Increase Risk: In Georgia, filing a property tax appeal may result in the Board of Equalization increasing your property's assessed value. OverAssessed reviews all relevant market data before filing to minimize this risk, and we will not file an appeal if our analysis indicates a likely value increase.
❄️ 3-Year Freeze (O.C.G.A. § 48-5-299(c)): A successful appeal resolved at a Board of Equalization hearing freezes your assessed value for three years (current year + two additional years). Values settled informally with the assessor's office may not qualify for the three-year freeze.
📊 85/15 Rule: Under Georgia's 85/15 rule, your first-year tax bill may reflect limited savings as the county retains a portion of the reduction. Full savings are typically realized in years 2 and 3 of the freeze period. Our fee is calculated only on actual Year 1 tax savings you receive, not the total reduction amount.
💰 Fee Calculation: Our fee of 25% is applied only to the actual property tax savings you receive in the year of the appeal. If we do not achieve a reduction, you pay nothing. No upfront fees, no admin fees, no hidden costs.
⚖️ Not Legal Advice: OverAssessed, LLC provides property tax consulting services and is not a law firm. Our services do not constitute legal advice. We act as your authorized agent for the property tax appeal process under Georgia law.
📋 No Guarantee: Past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Each property tax appeal is evaluated on its individual merits. Success rates and savings amounts vary by property and county.
✍️ Agent Authorization: By signing this agreement, you authorize OverAssessed, LLC to act as your agent for your property tax appeal. This includes filing PT-311A and representing you before the Board of Tax Assessors and Board of Equalization.
