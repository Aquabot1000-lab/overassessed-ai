Frequently Asked Questions

Everything Georgia homeowners need to know

❄️ What is the 3-year value freeze?

Under Georgia law (O.C.G.A. § 48-5-299(c)), when a property owner wins an appeal at a Board of Equalization hearing, the assessed value is frozen at the reduced amount for the current year plus the next two tax years. This means one appeal can save you money for three consecutive years — no need to refile.

⚠️ Can my value go up if I appeal?

Yes — in Georgia, the Board of Equalization has the authority to increase your assessed value during an appeal. That's why we carefully screen every property before filing. If our analysis shows your home may be at risk of an increase, we won't file the appeal. Your protection is our priority.

💰 How is the fee calculated?

We charge 25% of your actual Year 1 property tax savings only. If we reduce your assessed value and you save $500 in taxes in Year 1, our fee is $125 — period. Years 2 and 3 of the freeze are free. If we don't save you money, you pay nothing. No upfront fees, no admin fees, no hidden costs.

📋 What is the 85/15 rule?

Georgia's 85/15 rule means your first-year tax bill may reflect limited savings as the county retains a portion of the reduction. Full savings are typically realized in Years 2 and 3 of the freeze period. Our fee is calculated only on what you actually save in Year 1.

⏰ When should I file?

You have 45 days from the date of your assessment notice to file a PT-311A appeal. We recommend signing up as soon as you receive your notice — the sooner we start, the stronger your case. Don't wait until the deadline!

Important Disclosures

⚠️ Value Increase Risk
In Georgia, filing a property tax appeal may result in the Board of Equalization increasing your property's assessed value. OverAssessed reviews all relevant market data before filing to minimize this risk, and we will not file an appeal if our analysis indicates a likely value increase.
❄️ 3-Year Freeze (O.C.G.A. § 48-5-299(c))
A successful appeal resolved at a Board of Equalization hearing freezes your assessed value for three years (current year + two additional years) under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-299(c). Values settled informally with the assessor's office may not qualify for the three-year freeze.
📊 85/15 Rule
Under Georgia's 85/15 rule, your first-year tax bill may reflect limited savings as the county retains a portion of the reduction. Full savings are typically realized in years 2 and 3 of the freeze period. Our fee is calculated only on actual Year 1 tax savings you receive, not the total reduction amount.
